About Me

I'm a 25-year-old who loves computers, history, and eSports. Most recently I have gotten into watching a few different kinds of esports or "Electronic Sports."

Beyond gaming and esports, I played some sports in elementary and middle school. When I was six years old to about 10 years old I played baseball on a local team! I didn't have a position since we were still basically learning the game to have fun, but I did hit a few good Home Runs! A few years later I delved into my first experience of Track and Field. I found out very quickly that I wasn't a runner or a jumper... However I did find that Shotput and Discus were right up my alley! Possible thanks to my experiencce in Baseball maybe? Who knows! I only did one season though as the next season ended abruptly thanks to a broken arm of mine...

After I had finished baseball, I found myself less interested in sports and more interested in Music! I picked up the violin at 11-years-old and played all the way through my Senior year of High School! Through my time playing an insturment in a group setting, I learned a lot about confidence, cohesion, and cooperation. As tacky as that sounds, playing an instrument in front of a hundred or more people takes a lot of confidence. In order to be heard, you have to play as loud as the rest of the orchestra; and trust me, the audience can hear your mistakes. Every player is a cog in the machine of the music and the conductor is merely the driver behind the wheel. If the musicians are ill-prepared and ignorant to the piece they're playing, the entire ensemble can fall apart from one musician's lack of practice.

In my time playing the violin I also learned a lot of history since so many pieces were written in different eras and played for certain events. Of course, the most famous orchestral ensembles are from Europe.

Expanding beyond than just musical history, I'm a sucker for war history between WWI and WWII. However, I've never been outside of the country. I've always wanted to go somewhere where I can actually stand where a pivotal historical battle or opera took place. The more history I learn the more I want to go to those places to compare the photos, instruments, and vehicles and truly appreciate how far we, as a civilization, have come since both the beautiful and awful events took place.

TrailheadZ

This is my first ever project I worked on with a group. I'm very proud of the work put into this and look forward to working with others on similar projects!

TrailheadZ is a web application that takes advantage of a couple different APIs. The first being REI's Hiking Project API and Google's own Maps and Geolocation API.

Another key feature that we wanted to include was a way for veteran and new hikers to connect with each other. With knowledge of JSON, we were able to use Reddit's Colorado Hikers subreddit to implement a similar feature.
